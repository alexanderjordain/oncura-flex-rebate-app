## 1. Executive summary

FLEX is a prepaid telemedicine financing program. Veterinary clinics sign a contract that
entitles them to a fixed quarterly pool of telemedicine services (rads + ultrasound scans
read by Oncura's specialists). They pay the pool down in monthly installments — usually
through a third-party finance company — and consume the pool through actual scans throughout
the quarter. At quarter end, the books reconcile: any unused portion of the pool is
recognized as revenue; any usage over the pool is billed as overage.

**Three motions, three timings:**

| Motion | Frequency | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| Finance-co payment + monthly credit memo | Monthly | Builds the credit pool on the clinic's QBO account |
| OPD scan activity | Daily | Burns down the pool via individual scan invoices |
| Quarter-end reconciliation | Per clinic's quarter | Absorbs unused balance OR bills overage |

---

## 2. The problem FLEX solves

Telemedicine reads are expensive on a per-scan basis. A small clinic that only needs a few
scans per month can't predict their costs, and Oncura can't predict its revenue.

FLEX converts an unpredictable per-scan model into a **predictable subscription**:
- Clinic pays a fixed monthly amount via a finance partner (or self-funds it).
- In exchange, the clinic gets a fixed quarterly "pool" of services they can consume.
- Cost stops being a barrier to ordering the right scan — they've already paid for it.
- Oncura gets predictable recurring revenue and a smoother specialist workload.

---

## 3. Who's in the system

**Clinic (the customer)** — a veterinary practice that wants predictable access to Oncura's
telemedicine reads. They are the QBO customer of record.

**Finance company (most clinics)** — usually one of:
- **OnePlace Capital** — contract numbers begin `OPC` + a digit string
- **Great America (GA)** — Maintenance-charge remittances; contract identifier is the Payment Invoice Number
- **New Lane** — sends a "consumables" file combining FLEX + scan-package payments

The finance company takes the credit risk: they pay Oncura monthly per the contract, and
the clinic owes the finance company directly. Oncura is paid regardless of whether the
clinic pays the finance company on time.

**Self-funded clinics** — a smaller set of clinics on FLEX without a finance partner. They
pay Oncura directly at the quarterly cadence.

**Oncura** — the service provider and the revenue recognizer. Generates monthly credit memos
that build the clinic's credit pool; runs the quarter-end reconciliation that recognizes
revenue.

---

## 4. The monthly cash flow — a worked example

A representative clinic with a `monthly_credit` of $1,000 and a `quarterly_threshold` of $6,000.
(These two numbers come from the clinic's FLEX Master row.)

**On the 1st of each month:**

1. The finance company wires Oncura the contract's monthly amount — say, $1,000.
   - Recorded in QBO as a **Receive Payment** on the clinic's account.
   - Intentionally **left unapplied** (per Cash SOP-9). It sits as a credit on the clinic's account.

2. Oncura creates a **monthly credit memo** on the same clinic's account, also for $1,000.
   - Item: `Flex-credits`, Class: `03-Telemedicine`
   - Dated to the last day of the prior month (Marty's convention)
   - Generated by the FLEX app's Stage 2 cycle, one per ledger payment row.

After both motions, the clinic's QBO account has **two unapplied credits totaling $2,000**.

**Throughout the month:**

3. Each scan the clinic orders generates an invoice in QBO at the retail rate.
4. These invoices accumulate as A/R.

**At quarter end:**

5. The Accounting Manager runs the quarterly reconciliation (Accounting SOP-11):
   - Un-apply all the auto-matched payment/credit applications.
   - Manually re-apply in the pattern: payment → credit → payment → credit, month by month.
   - The 6 total credits ($2,000 × 3 months for the 1× clinic, or $1,000 × 6 entries depending
     on how you count them — same totals) burn down against the quarter's scan invoices.
   - Whatever's left is either unused (negative balance) or overage (positive balance).

---

## 5. Why two credit entries each month (the trickiest part)

**Each month the clinic's account gets credited TWICE for the same FLEX entitlement:**

- The **finance-company payment** (cash actually wired to Oncura)
- The **monthly credit memo** (a bookkeeping entry of the same dollar amount)

This is the part most people miss on first reading. The two together — payment + credit
memo — are what funds the clinic's quarterly service entitlement. Roughly:

```
monthly_credit (per FLEX Master)            ≈ $1,000
+ matching finance-co payment that month    ≈ $1,000
= per-month spending power                  ≈ $2,000

× 3 months in a quarter                     = $6,000 ≈ quarterly_threshold
```

The ratio isn't always exactly 2:1 (Chenango: 1.93×; Alum Rock: 2.23×) but it's always
close. The asymmetry reflects contract-specific terms negotiated with each finance partner.

**Why does the system work this way?** Because the finance company is paying Oncura wholesale
for the FLEX program, while the threshold is denominated at retail (the cost the clinic
would otherwise pay per scan). The credit memo bridges that gap on the clinic's QBO account.

---

## 6. Quarter-end reconciliation — three possible outcomes

After the Accounting Manager applies all the quarter's payments and credits against the
quarter's scan invoices, one of three things is true:

### Outcome A: Zero balance (clean quarter)
Scans consumed ≈ pool size. Account reconciles to zero. No further action.

### Outcome B: Positive remaining balance — overage
Scans consumed > pool size. The clinic owes Oncura the difference.
- If the finance partner handles overages (OnePlace, by their cutoff date, usually the 5th of
  the month): send the overage amount to the partner.
- If the finance partner declines (Great America, New Lane) OR the cutoff was missed: Oncura
  creates a **direct overage invoice** addressed to the clinic. Reference: "Telemedicine
  Overages" + the quarter.
- Pre-existing credit balances (from prior-quarter overpayments) get applied to current
  overages first.

### Outcome C: Negative balance — unused funds
Scans consumed < pool size. The clinic has a credit balance on their account that needs to
be converted into recognized revenue (it can't sit indefinitely).
- Oncura creates an **unused-Flex-credits invoice** (the app's Stage 3 recapture) on the
  clinic's account for exactly the unused amount.
- This invoice consumes the credit balance and recognizes the revenue.
- **The invoice is NOT mailed to the clinic** — it's an internal accounting entry. The clinic
  already paid for the capacity through the finance company; recapture just acknowledges
  that the unused portion is still earned revenue.

## 7. Multi-clinic groups

Some clinics are organized as multi-location entities under a single FLEX contract. They
reconcile as one pool, with funds moving between locations to balance the group.

**Known groups (as of 2026):**

| Group | Anchor location | Member locations |
|---|---|---|
| **Mohnacky** | Carlsbad | Vista, Escondido |
| **River Trail** | Tulsa | Memorial |
| **PR-vets** | Clinica Gardenville | Acuario, Diaz Umpierre, La Muda, Condado |

**How reallocation works (River Trail / Mohnacky / PR-vets pattern):**
1. Apply each month's payments to the location they came in on.
2. After the quarter, check each location's individual balance.
3. Move funds across locations under the same contract:
   - Example: PR-vets — $978 moved from Condado to Gardenville (Condado unused, Gardenville
     overage)
4. The group's contract pool = monthly entitlement × number of active locations.

The app handles this automatically: in Stage 3, group anchors are the only rows that emit
recapture invoices; member-location activity rolls up into the anchor's totals.

## 8. SOPs that govern FLEX

| SOP | Title | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash SOP-9** | FLEX Finance Co Payment Import | Receiving the monthly remittance and entering it in QBO/SaasAnt |
| **Cash SOP-10** | NewLane Pass-Through | NewLane-specific file processing (FLEX + scan mixed in one file) |
| **Accounting SOP-5** | FLEX Credit Memo Import | Generating the monthly credit memos via SaasAnt |
| **Accounting SOP-10** | Flex Pass-Through Credit Memo Application | Manual catch-up for missing or multi-payment-month credits |
| **Accounting SOP-11** | Flex Account Reconciliation | The quarter-end walkthrough per clinic |
| **Accounting SOP-12** | Flex Overage Billing — Internal Process | Routing overages to finance partner vs. direct invoice |
| **Accounting SOP-13** | Multi-Clinic Flex Contract Management | River Trail / Mohnacky / PR-vets reconciliation |
| **Accounting SOP-14** | Funds Reallocation Across Clinics | Mechanics of moving credits between linked locations |
| **Accounting SOP-15** | Account Locking | Locking FLEX accounts at quarter close, monthly unlock |

## 9. Three calendar spreads (staggered quarters)

Not every clinic closes its quarter at the same time. The FLEX Master assigns each clinic to
one of three calendars:

| Calendar spread | Quarter-end months |
|---|---|
| `Calendar` | March, June, September, December |
| `March-April-May` | February, May, August, November |
| `May-June-July` | January, April, July, October |

This is what spreads the Accounting Manager's quarter-end workload across every month
instead of bunching it into four heavy weeks.

The app's Stage 3 only emits recapture/overage rows for clinics whose quarter **ends in the
selected target month**.

---

## 10. The program is closed

As of 2026, FLEX is **closed to new entrants**. The active clinic list only shrinks (when
contracts complete or clinics churn). New telemedicine partnerships use a different
arrangement.

**Implication for the app:** the master list is mostly stable; changes are deactivations
(contract end) rather than additions. Don't expect a "create new FLEX clinic" workflow.

---

## 11. Glossary (for slides)

- **Pool / quarterly_threshold** — the total dollar value of services a clinic can consume
  per quarter under FLEX.
- **monthly_credit** — half (roughly) of the monthly contribution to the pool; the value of
  the credit memo Oncura issues each month.
- **Pass-through payment** — the finance company's monthly cash payment, applied as an
  unapplied credit on the clinic's QBO account.
- **Recapture / unused-Flex-credits invoice** — internal accounting entry at quarter-end
  that converts unused pool into recognized revenue. Not sent to the clinic.
- **Overage** — a real bill, either submitted to the finance partner or mailed directly to
  the clinic, when scan activity exceeds the pool.
- **Anchor / group anchor** — in a multi-clinic group, the location whose row in FLEX
  Master holds the consolidated pool. Other locations are members.

# Brand System — for the deck designer

The deck must match the FLEX + Rebate Ledger app's visual identity. The app uses a calm,
financial-instrument aesthetic — not consumer-y, not corporate-stiff. The palette is steel
blue (trust + data), leaf green (positive outcomes), amber (attention/warnings), on a cool
grey canvas with white "card" surfaces.

## Color tokens

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `canvas` | `#F0F2F4` | Slide background (cool grey) |
| `surface` | `#FFFFFF` | Card / panel backgrounds |
| `ink` | `#2A3742` | Body text (dark slate) |
| `blue` | `#3A6A9A` | Primary — headings, links, key emphasis |
| `blue_deep` | `#2F567E` | Hover / active blue, deeper emphasis |
| `green` | `#469B68` | Positive data, reconciled outcomes, success |
| `amber` | `#E3A033` | Kickers, accents, warnings, attention |
| `muted` | `#6B7785` | Captions, secondary labels |
| `line` | `#E2E6EA` | Hairline dividers, card borders |

**Color usage rules:**
- Body text is `ink` on `canvas` or `surface`. Never blue body text.
- Headings are `blue` in serif. Always.
- Kickers (small uppercase eyebrow text above a heading) are `amber` in mono, letter-spaced.
- Numeric figures (dollars, counts, percentages) are `blue` in mono, tabular figures.
- Positive outcomes (reconciled, clean quarter) signal with `green`.
- Warnings, attention-needed, or amber-coded SOPs signal with `amber`.
- Hairline dividers between cards / sections use `line`.

## Type system

| Family | Use | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Fraunces** (display serif) | Slide titles, section headings | 500–700 |
| **Hanken Grotesk** (sans) | Body, captions, lists, labels | 400 body / 600 emphasis |
| **IBM Plex Mono** (mono) | All financial figures, refs, dates, code, kickers | 400–600 |

Google Fonts URL:
```
https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Fraunces:opsz,wght@9..144,400;9..144,500;9..144,600;9..144,700&family=Hanken+Grotesk:wght@400;500;600;700&family=IBM+Plex+Mono:wght@400;500;600&display=swap
```

**Type rules:**
- Slide titles: Fraunces 600, color `blue`, size ~44pt, letter-spacing -0.01em.
- Section headings: Fraunces 600, `blue`, ~28pt.
- Body: Hanken Grotesk 400, `ink`, ~18pt.
- Tables, dollar figures, contract numbers, dates: IBM Plex Mono, tabular-nums, `blue` for headlines, `ink` for body cells.
- Eyebrow kickers above titles: IBM Plex Mono uppercase, letter-spacing 0.28em, ~10pt, `amber`.

## Layout signature elements

- **Header block**: a 4px left-rule in `green` runs the full height of the title block. Below it, an `amber` kicker, then the Fraunces title, then a `muted` sub-line. Always.
- **Metric cards**: white background, 1px `line` border, 3px left-rule in `blue`, slight shadow (rgba(42,55,66,.05) 1px 3px). Label in mono uppercase letter-spaced. Value in mono blue tabular-nums.
- **Section dividers**: hairline `line` color, full width.
- **No gradients** except very subtle background mesh: two large radial gradients (`blue` 6% top-right, `green` 5% top-left) on the canvas. Almost imperceptible.
- **No emoji, no stock photography.** Use icons (line-weight 1.5px) in `blue` or `ink` where helpful.

## Voice / copy guidelines

- Direct, not breezy. "The pool absorbs the unused balance" beats "Don't worry — we got you covered!"
- Concrete numbers when possible. "$1,000 monthly entitlement" beats "your monthly amount".
- Reference the SOP by number when describing a workflow ("Per Accounting SOP-11 …")
- Never say "easy" or "simple" — financial workflows aren't, and saying so undermines the operator.
- Match the app's existing on-screen voice (see captions in `pages/flex_cycle.py` for examples).
